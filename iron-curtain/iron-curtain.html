About the Game

A competitive two-player card game set during the Cold War era. Inspired by the Cold War conflict between two nations, each round players choose weapon and economy cards to outplay their opponent. The game rewards both strategic planning and reading your opponent's hand.

Inspiration

Inspired by Papers Please's political tension between two nations, and Sheriff of Nottingham's hidden information mechanics. The rock-paper-scissors card matching system gave the game its core rhythm.

Brainstorm

Originally planned as a multiplayer game, I chose a two-player format for a tighter, more engaging experience. Early sketches explored board layouts, card flow between offense and defense zones, and the Cold War framing.

Game Flow

The full game flow covers drawing cards, placing attack and defense cards, resolving outcomes, and handling debuffs. Each round loops through this sequence until one player's health coins drop to zero.

Card Design

Two card categories represent essential war factors — green (economy/resources) and red (weapons). Each category has three power tiers. The visual language carries rough, hand-drawn energy to match the game's wartime tone.

Game Components

Each player starts with 12 health coins — lose them all and you lose the game. Blank "permanent" cards add a bluffing layer, letting players stall or deceive opponents by placing empty cards in both zones.

Sample Play

Players take turns drawing cards from the deck. In the first round each player draws 8 cards (including blanks), and 6 cards in every round after. Both players place cards simultaneously in offensive and defensive zones — meaning you must predict what your opponent will do.

Board & Packaging

The game box and foldout rulebook cover setup, card types, and sample plays — with the same red-blue cordoned visual language carried through from the board itself.
