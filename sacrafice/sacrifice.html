About the Game

A single-player puzzle game where you control two robots with different abilities. Inspired by Shadow Tactics: Blades of the Shogun and the sci-fi world of Altered Carbon, players must coordinate both robots to push boxes onto targets and clear each level within a limited number of steps.

The core concept: sometimes sacrificing one robot's position is the only way to move forward.

Inspiration

The cooperative movement system draws from Shadow Tactics, while the sci-fi setting is inspired by Altered Carbon. The top-down puzzle structure is a modern take on classic Sokoban.

Level Design

Levels were sketched on paper first — mapping out box positions, robot start points, and target locations. Each level was designed around a specific mechanic to teach or challenge the player.

Early Prototyping

Early builds used placeholder cylinders and white boxes to test robot movement and box pushing mechanics before finalising the art style.

3D Modeling

Both robot characters were modeled in 3ds Max. Two color variations — yellow/blue and teal — distinguish the two playable robots. Custom loop animations were created for idle and movement states.

Programming

The player controller handles robot switching, movement direction, box detection, and step counting. Written in C# inside Unity, with coroutines managing the smooth movement animations.
